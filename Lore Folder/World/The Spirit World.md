# The Spirit World

## Nature

The Spirit World is not another dimension or an afterlife. It is a parallel layer of the same world — the same geography, the same mountains and coasts and forests, but inhabited by a different order of existence. Where the material world is governed by mortality and physical matter, the Spirit World is governed by essence. Its inhabitants are spirits with shape — beings who exist as pure selfhood, without the threat of death by age or disease or decay.

Nobody truly dies in the Spirit World. They can be diminished, wounded, transformed — but the end that material beings face does not exist there.

## Inhabitants

The Spirit World is home to magical creatures and humanoid beings of various kinds. They are not human and do not look it.

**Appearance:** Spirit World beings are immediately distinguishable from material world beings by two features — their eyes, which are layered like the painted eyes of a traditional Barong mask, deep and concentric and not entirely still; and their ears, long and sharp, tapering to a point. These are not variations. Every being native to the Spirit World carries both.

## The Divine Lion

The Spirit World Forest — the largest and oldest region of the Spirit World — is ruled by the Divine Lion, Barong. He is not merely powerful; he is one of the most significant figures in the Spirit World's order, a being of protection and balance whose domain covers the forest and whose authority extends far beyond it.

For eight hundred years, the kings of Devata channeled his power through the Barong mask to seal [[Rangda]]. Barong permitted this. The ritual was real, the purpose was right, and the arrangement held.

What the kings did not tend to — what none of them examined closely enough — was their own atma.

## The Veil and Its Breaking

The veil between the Spirit World and the material world is not a wall. It is a membrane, maintained by the spiritual health of those who cross or channel between them. The sealing ritual, performed correctly with a whole atma, reinforces it. Performed carelessly — with a corrupted atma, with a king who treated Barong's power as a tool and never looked inward — it fed that corruption directly to the Lion and into the boundary between worlds.

Eight hundred years of kings. Eight hundred years of atma corruption accumulated and passed through the mask, into Barong, into the veil.

The veil did not break all at once. It thinned. It softened. It developed places where the membrane no longer held — where a Spirit World being could slip through without meaning to, where crossings that required intention began happening by accident.

By the time King Dharma refused the mask, the veil was already compromised. [[Rangda]]'s victory and the collapse of the cycle did not cause the thinning — it only ended the last thing that might have eventually repaired it.

## Materialization

A spirit being that remains in the material world too long begins to develop a physical body. This is not a gift. The body forms around them shaped by their nature and their environment — it is autonomous, it hungers, it defends itself, it acts on instinct. The spirit inside becomes a passenger in something that was never meant to exist.

The only way to release a materialized spirit is to destroy the physical body. The material form dissolves. The spirit returns to where it belongs.

This is understood among those who know the old ways. It is an act of mercy, not violence.

Nobody is keeping count of how many are lost.
