# The Throne

## What It Is

The Throne of Devata is not a symbol of power. It is the mechanism of it. Whoever sits on it becomes the land's ruler in a literal sense — their nature, their atma, their corruption or wholeness radiates outward into Devata itself. The land breathes through its ruler.

This is not something the ruler does consciously. It is not a spell or a ritual. It simply happens. The throne is the conduit and the seated ruler is the source.

## How It Works

The ruler's inner state becomes the land's condition. A king of clean atma and just rule produces a Devata that reflects it — fertile, stable, the veil between worlds intact, sacred sites functioning as they should. The land does not reward goodness. It mirrors it.

The inverse is equally true. A corrupted ruler produces a corrupted land. Not gradually, not subtly — the rot spreads from the throne outward, soaking into soil, water, air. Everything the land touches is touched by what the ruler is.

## Under Rangda

[[Rangda]] has sat on the throne for two hundred years.

Two centuries of her nature bleeding into Devata. The corruption is not localized — it is everywhere, in everything, inescapable for anyone living within the land's reach. It affects the people, their atma, their capacity for wholeness. It affects the sacred sites that once served as stable crossing points between worlds.

It affects the Spirit World beings who drifted through the broken veil. Their material bodies — built from whatever the land provides — are made of corrupted matter. The environment that assembled them was already rotten. They did not become aggressive. They were built aggressive, from the ground up, out of two hundred years of [[Rangda]]'s throne.

## The Implication

Unseating [[Rangda]] is not enough. Killing her is not enough on its own. What matters is what sits on the throne afterward — because Devata will begin reflecting that instead. The land does not heal on its own. It waits for a ruler to heal through.
