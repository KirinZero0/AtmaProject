# The Barong Mask

## Origin

The mask began as a regal accessory — ceremonial, decorative, worn by the first king who stood against Rangda. It was not yet what it would become.

Baradha changed that during the battle itself. Dying, with no other option, he pushed the power of the Spirit Forest King into the mask — forcing a fragment of Barong's consciousness and strength into the object. He did not survive the act. He died before he could explain what he had done or what it would cost.

The first king did not know. He put the mask on and defeated Rangda. The cycle began without anyone understanding what they had started.

## What It Is

The mask is not the Divine Lion. It is a clone of Barong's consciousness — carrying a portion of his power and his nature, but not the full being. It thinks, it speaks, it has presence and will. It is not a tool.

It maintains a direct connection to the real Barong in the Spirit World. What the mask experiences, what it consumes, what it carries — the Lion knows. This connection is what made eight hundred years of corrupted atma a problem not just for the mask but for Barong himself.

## Form

The mask has two states. Unused, it is compact — a mask in the conventional sense, worn or carried, dormant but present. When a host puts it on, it blooms. It opens, expands, becomes something larger than it was. When it is done it reverts.

Even unused, even in its compact form, the mask grants power to whoever carries it — as long as it remains close. Reduced, but real. The host does not need to wear it to feel it.

## The Resurrection

The mask will not let its host die.

When the host is killed, the body turns to embers and reconstructs in a safe location. The host returns whole. This has no ceiling — it will keep bringing them back, again and again, for as long as they wear it.

The price is atma. Each resurrection takes a fragment of the host's soul — not a feeling, not a memory, the actual substance of what they are. The fragment goes to the mask. The mask feeds it to the Lion.

For eight hundred years, no king examined this closely. They were invincible. That was enough. King Dharma was the first to notice — not through knowledge or research, but through feeling. Something kept going missing. He kept coming back and each time he was slightly less himself. He could not name what was gone. He knew something was.

He refused to don the mask. He faced Rangda without it. He lost.

## Weapons and Armor

The mask can materialize weapons and armor from Barong's power — made real, physical, usable. Notable weapons carried by past kings include a greatsword, gauntlet, dagger, and bow.

Manifestation requires a conduit. Specifically a keris — the sacred dagger serves as the anchor through which the mask's power takes physical form. Without the keris, the weapons cannot be called.

## The Accumulated Dead

The mask retains the memories of every host it has ever carried. Not their personalities, not their voices — their combat knowledge. Eight hundred years of kings, their fighting instincts, their techniques, their muscle memory, all of it preserved inside the mask and accessible to whoever wears it.

A new host does not start from nothing. They start from everyone who came before.

## Wearing It

When the mask is placed on a host's face, two consciousnesses occupy one body. The host and the Lion's clone — present simultaneously, aware of each other, neither fully in control. The mask does not possess. It coexists.

It speaks rarely. It asks before acting, when it can. Whether that asking constitutes genuine consent or the shape of consent is a question the mask never answers directly.

It also grants superhuman strength to whoever wears it. The body becomes more than it was. This is not subtle.

## What It Wants

The mask chose [[Prince Natha]]. It waited two hundred years after Dharma's refusal for the right bloodline to arrive. It identified him the moment the conspirators placed it on his face — recognized the lineage, recognized the potential, and decided.

Whether that decision was the mask's or the Lion's is unclear. The connection between them runs deep enough that the distinction may not matter.
