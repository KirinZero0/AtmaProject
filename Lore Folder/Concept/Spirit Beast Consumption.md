# Spirit Beast Consumption

---

## What Spirit Beasts Are

Spirit beasts are animals of the Spirit World — not humanoid, not divine in the way that Dedari or Garuda are, but beings of that world nonetheless. What distinguishes them from ordinary animals is origin, not form. They look like animals. They move like animals. A villager who saw one in the forest would not know what they were looking at.

Like all Spirit World beings that cross into the material world, they do not persist unchanged. A spirit beast that cannot return — lost, stranded, caught when the veil closed — begins to accumulate material substance. Built from whatever the land it occupies offers. In Devata, the land has been corrupted for centuries: eight hundred years of tainted atma, two hundred years of Rangda's occupation. What the land offers is not clean.

The material body that forms around a stranded spirit beast is built from that. The animal that results looks wrong in ways that are difficult to name — too still, or too loud, or off in the way that a familiar thing is when the light hits it wrong. It is still recognizable as an animal. It is not only an animal anymore.

## How Consumption Works

Eating spirit beast meat introduces Spirit World substance directly into a human body. This is not a spiritual metaphor. The meat carries what the spirit beast is — and what the corrupted material world made it.

The body does not reject it. The body does not know to reject it. It processes the meat like any food, absorbs what it absorbs, and the change begins.

### Unprocessed Consumption (Raw Meat)

The progression is consistent across every documented case:

**Weeks 1–2 — Physical changes begin**
The eyes change first. They bulge slightly — the pressure behind them building in ways the socket was not made to accommodate. The iris takes on a quality specific to Spirit World denizens: too defined, too present. The skin begins to pale, not from illness but from something happening beneath the surface.

**Weeks 2–4 — Skeletal mutation**
Bone structure changes. Protrusions emerge — horn-like formations pushing outward from the skull, the shoulders, the spine. These are not additions. They are mutations of existing bone, the skeleton rearranging itself under the influence of what has been consumed. The process is not painless. By the end of this stage the pain has stopped, which is worse.

**Week 4 onward — Consciousness loss**
The brain is affected last and most completely. The consumed person does not die. What happens is more precise: they lose access to their own consciousness. The consciousness remains — it is still there, still present, still registering everything that happens to the body. It simply cannot reach the surface. Cannot speak. Cannot move on its own intention. Cannot refuse.

The person inside is aware. They are watching. They cannot do anything about it.

**Ongoing — Regeneration**
The consumed body regenerates at a rate that cannot be overcome. Wounds close. Limbs repair. There is no injury severe enough to permanently damage what the spirit beast substance has made of the body. The consumed cannot be killed. They cannot be freed by destruction. The only cure is the source of the corruption itself — the broken veil that allowed the spirit beasts to become what they became in Devata's corrupted land.

## How It Spread Through the Middle Ground

Rangda did not discover spirit beast consumption by accident. Her disciples studied it. They understood, before the villages did, what would happen.

The sequence was deliberate:

Rangda banned hunting across the Middle Ground after taking the kingdom. The stated reason was resource management — game would be tracked and harvested by designated parties, the yield directed to the capital. The actual reason was to remove the villages' food independence and force them toward a single alternative.

The crops were taxed to the capital immediately after harvest. What remained for the villages was not enough.

The forests still had spirit beasts. Spirit beasts that looked like animals. In hunger, with their usual game off-limits and their harvests gone, the villages hunted what was available.

Some ate what they caught. The change takes weeks. By the time the first cases showed their symptoms, the meat had already moved through the village — shared, stored, cooked into communal meals. The whole village ate before anyone understood what was happening. And by the time the understanding came, it was already too late.

The spread to full village conversion happened within weeks once it started. It was not an outbreak. It was the conclusion of a plan.

## Control: The Overseer System

The consumed villagers are not automatically obedient to Rangda. They are mindless — they will perform repetitive physical labor without direction, but they cannot follow complex instruction and they cannot be organized without external control.

Rangda addresses this through the Overseer system. Each converted village is assigned:

**A Noble Overseer** — drawn from Narendra's administrative class. These are collaborators: people who understood which way the occupation was going and chose a position within it over the alternative. The Overseer does not govern the village in any ordinary sense. Their function is the puppet strings.

The black magic that Overseers practice is a diluted derivation of Rangda's own puppet ability — the same principle that allows Rangda to control bodies from a distance, scaled down and made applicable to the specific condition of the consumed. Because the consumed have lost access to their own consciousness, the channel that was occupied by their will is empty. The Overseer's strings fill it. The consumed follow direction the way a hand follows the arm moving it.

**Rangda's Soldiers** — stationed at each village to manage the Overseer, maintain supply routing, and handle anything that requires a decision the puppet strings cannot make. The soldiers are administrators at this point. The workforce does not require disciplining. It does not complain. It does not rest. It does not need to be fed much, because the spirit beast transformation has restructured what the body requires.

This is what Satya watches from Hunter Camp A. His village. His neighbors. Working the terraces from before dawn to after dark, feeding the capital that consumed them.

## Processed Consumption — The Cliff Temple Experiments

Rangda's disciples did not stop at observing what raw consumption produced. They asked what else spirit beast substance could be made to do if it was processed, refined, and introduced to human subjects under controlled conditions rather than through ordinary digestion.

The experiments at the Cliff Temple are the result of that question.

The disciples are not working with raw meat. They are working with extracted spirit beast material — broken down to its fundamental components, recombined with specific intent. The goal was flight, because the logistics problem between the Middle Ground and Narendra required an aerial solution. They found a Garuda stranded in the temple. They studied its material body. They extracted what made that body capable of flight and introduced it into human subjects.

The mechanism is not biological. The flight capability in Garuda's material body is a property of corruption — of what corrupted land builds when it has Spirit World substance to work with. What the disciples are extracting and introducing into humans is that corruption, targeted and directed.

The successful experiments work because the corruption took hold in the right way. The subjects that survived and stabilized can fly — functional enough for cargo transport, compliant enough to follow routing instructions. They were removed from the facility and deployed in Rangda's military logistics chain. They exist. They work.

The failures are simply the ones the corruption destroyed faster than it built.

What the disciples do not understand — and have no framework to understand — is that scaling this process requires not better technique but more corruption. The successful rate is a function of how much Devata's land has already been degraded. It will not improve until the land degrades further.

## The Village Chief — Voluntary Consumption

The Mutated Village Chief of the Shore is the only known case of voluntary spirit beast consumption.

He knew what spirit beasts were. He knew, from whatever sources he had access to, that consuming one would change him. What he believed — what he chose to believe, because the alternative was watching the occupation take his village without resistance — was that his will was strong enough to hold against it. That he could take the power and keep himself.

He was wrong. But the voluntary nature of his consumption left something the involuntary cases do not have: a thread. His consciousness did not lose access completely. It surfaces in moments — during the fight with Natha, in the pauses between attacks, in the muscle memory of the dagger forms his hands remember before his mind has caught up. He is not gone. He is further under than he can fight his way back from, most of the time.

Those moments of recognition are what make the fight what it is. Not a monster that needs to be put down. A man who made a wrong choice for the right reasons, still present enough to know what is happening to him.

## Reversal

The consumption cannot be reversed by any means available within the occupation. The disciples have no interest in reversing it. The consumed themselves cannot reach far enough through the corruption to find a way back. There is no treatment, no medicine, no ritual practice that can undo what spirit beast substance has done to a human body while Devata's veil remains broken.

The veil broke during the Calonarang Incident. Rangda's occupation and the corruption it has sustained have kept it broken. Spirit beasts continue to materialize in the corrupted land. The consumed continue to regenerate from that same corruption.

The only reversal is the source.

When Natha makes peace with Barong and takes the throne — when the broken veil is restored — the ground of Devata changes. The corruption that sustained the spirit beasts and sustained the transformations has no source anymore. The changes the consumed underwent were built from that corruption. Without it, the building material dissolves.

The consumed return.

This is what Satya is waiting for. He cannot explain exactly why he believes it is possible. He simply does not accept the alternative. He is right.
