# Atma

## What It Is

Atma is the essence of mortal life. Not a metaphor — a literal substance. It takes the shape of a blue flame, visible to those who know how to look, and it is what a person truly is. Not their body, not their memories, not their name. The atma underneath all of it.

Every living being carries one. It is present from birth and it persists until the being ceases to exist entirely.

## Purity and Corruption

Atma is born pure. A newborn's atma is clean blue — undiluted, unweighed. 

As life continues, sin accumulates. Not guilt, not regret — sin in the direct sense, the actual weight of harmful choices and moral compromise. Each one leaves a mark on the atma. The flame dims and discolors. A life lived without care for what it costs others will produce an atma that is barely recognizable as what it started as.

This is not metaphor in this world. The corruption is real and visible. The atma is what a person truly is — which means a corrupted atma is an accurate portrait of who someone has become.

## Atma and the Spirit World

God-level spirit beings — beings of Barong's order — do not have atma. They are spirit without mortality, essence without the fragility that atma represents. They cannot be corrupted by their own nature.

What they can be corrupted by is someone else's.

## The Mask — Baradha's Invention

For most of history the idea of feeding atma to a divine being did not exist. It was not a practice, not a ritual, not even a concept. Mortal essence and divine essence occupied different orders entirely.

Baradha changed this. Through means not fully recorded, he found a way to weaponize the relationship between mortal atma and divine power — to channel one into the other, using a mask as the conduit. Wearing the mask, a mortal could draw on Barong's divine strength. The price was atma, fed through the mask to the Lion each time the power was used.

The kings who came after understood the power. They did not understand — or did not care about — what they were feeding.

## The Cost Over Eight Hundred Years

A king with a clean atma feeding small amounts to Barong across a reign would cause minimal damage. But no king lives without sin, and the ritual was performed in times of crisis, under pressure, with atma that had been accumulating the weight of rule — the compromises, the violence, the decisions that kings make and learn to live with.

Eight hundred years of this. Generation after generation of corrupted atma consumed by the Divine Lion, who was never designed to receive it, who had no defense against it because the concept of needing one had never existed before Baradha.

The veil thinned. Barong himself began to carry something that was never his.

The kings never looked. The ritual worked. That was enough.
