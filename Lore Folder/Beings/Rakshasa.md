# Rakshasa

### Beings Lore | Spirit World

---

## What They Are

Rakshasa are giant beings of the spirit world — massive, predatory, built for force in a way most spirit world beings are not. They are not soldiers or servants. They are apex presences in the spirit world's ecology, operating on a scale that makes most encounters with them one-sided.

They do not cross into the material world often. The material world is not their domain and they have no particular reason to enter it. When they do cross — when the veil thins enough or when something draws them through — the corrupted land builds them a material body the way it builds any stranded being's body: from what is available, shaped like what they are.

What the corrupted land builds for a Rakshasa is enormous.

---

## Appearance

Giant. The material body the corrupted land constructs for a Rakshasa reflects the being inside it — massive frame, disproportionate strength, a physical presence that changes the scale of any space it occupies. The face is not animal but is not entirely human either — broad, heavy-featured, built for the spirit world's different geometry.

They do not look like something that belongs in the material world. The corrupted land does its best with the template it is given and the result is something that does not fit comfortably in spaces designed for humans.

---

## In The World

Rakshasa do not appear as enemies in the game. Their presence in Devata's story is indirect — it is their cellular material that matters.

Rangda's disciples sourced Rakshasa material after establishing, through the Cliff Temple experiments, that spirit world cellular matter could be introduced into human subjects and produce functional enhancement. The question was which spirit world being provided material suited to military application.

Rakshasa provided force. The capital guard provided subjects.

The resulting enhanced soldiers — the Mutated Commanders, the Twin Gatekeepers — carry Rakshasa cellular material in frames that were originally human. The enhancement did not make them Rakshasa. It made them something that does not have a name yet, built from pieces of two things that were not designed to share a body.

---

## Notes

The Rakshasa whose material was sourced for the experiments are not named and do not appear. Whether they were killed in the sourcing process or whether the disciples found another method is not established. The moral weight of what was done to obtain the material is available in the lore for players who think about it. The game does not foreground it.

Rakshasa as beings are not inherently evil — "predatory" is not the same as malicious. They are what they are, operating in a world where force at their scale is simply their nature. What Rangda's disciples did with them was a different kind of violence than anything the Rakshasa themselves would have recognized.
