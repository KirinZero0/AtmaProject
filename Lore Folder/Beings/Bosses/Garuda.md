# Garuda

Garuda are divine beings of the Spirit World — humanoid in form, bird in nature. Winged. Built for altitude and distance in ways that material world creatures are not. In the Spirit World they move between its elevated reaches and its lower planes as intermediaries, carrying things — messages, presence, intent — across distances that ground-bound beings cannot traverse.

They are not angels in any theological sense. They are not messengers of the divine. They simply move, and the moving is what they are.

Like all Spirit World beings, they carry the marks of that world in their appearance: layered eyes like the deep rings of a Barong mask, sharp ears, a quality of stillness in the face that material world beings rarely achieve. Their wingspan is significant. In the Spirit World this is unremarkable. In the material world it is the first thing anyone notices and the last thing anyone forgets.

They are not meant to cross. When they do it is brief — passing through, following something, chasing something back. The material world is thicker than the Spirit World. Heavier. Garuda feel this immediately upon crossing. They were not built for weight.

They are not meant to stay.

---

## The One in the Cliff Temple

The Garuda found confined in the deepest chamber of the Cliff Temple has no name that the material world has record of. It crossed during a previous cycle — whether following something or simply passing through at the wrong moment is not known. The boundary closed. It could not return.

A Spirit World being stranded in the material world does not simply persist. It accumulates. The land presses into it — especially corrupted land, land that has absorbed eight hundred years of tainted atma and two centuries of Rangda's occupation. A body forms, built from what is available. For a Garuda, what formed was massive — material wing-structures, dense and heavy, nothing like the true wings underneath. A body shaped like what it was, built from what the corrupted land had to offer.

The aggression is the corruption's, not the Garuda's. The material body acts on what it is made of. The Garuda inside cannot stop it.

When Rangda's disciples arrived at the Cliff Temple, they did not capture a wild creature. They found something already trapped and built a cage around it. They identified the flight capability in the material body — the corrupted approximation of what a Garuda naturally is — and began the work of extracting and replicating it in human subjects.

The Garuda was aware of all of this. It remained aware throughout. Whatever consciousness persisted beneath the material body's aggression understood exactly what the disciples were doing and could not prevent any of it.

---

## Encounter

The Garuda does not want to fight. The body does. This distinction matters and changes nothing about the practical reality of the encounter.

Destroying the material form is the only way to end it. It is also the only liberation available — the only way to free what is underneath from what has been built around it.

When the body dissolves, the true form surfaces. Winged. Weightless in the way Spirit World beings are weightless — not without mass but without the heaviness the material world insists on. Present for a moment that stretches.

There is gratitude. Not performed. Not verbal. The specific gratitude of something that has been imprisoned in its own body for longer than it had any framework to measure, finally given the thing it could not give itself.

## The Mantra

Before ascending, the Garuda gives Natha the mantra for the Spectral Wings — the words that call the capacity for flight from the same source the Garuda drew from in its true form. Not a physical object. Not something taken. A transmission from something that no longer needs what it is giving away.

The Spectral Wings are not the Garuda's wings. They are the pattern of them — the intention, the reach, the movement between points that ground cannot connect. Natha carries it forward. The Garuda carries nothing back that it did not arrive with.

Then it ascends. The cage is empty. The cliff air comes through the gap in the containment wall where the material body, finally gone, no longer needs to be held.
