# Ancient Remnant

### Mob Document | Pura Adi — Pre-Split Civilization

---

## Overview

Warriors of the civilization that built Pura Adi — the people who existed before the split between the material world and the spirit world, who served Barong directly, and whose civilization ended when the split occurred. They did not survive the split. What remains in Pura Adi are echoes held in place by the same duty that held the Guardian: the last order they were given, still being carried out because no one came to relieve them.

They are not undead. They are not corrupted. They are remnants — present in the threshold because the threshold preserved everything that was inside it when the worlds divided. They have been here for millennia, doing the one thing they were told to do.

They are highly skilled. They were highly skilled when they were alive, and whatever the threshold has done to them in the time since has not diminished that. It has simply preserved it.

---

## Appearance

Large — the proportions of their civilization, built at a scale the current world does not produce. Humanoid, unmistakably so, but tall and broad in the way the doorways of Pura Adi are tall and broad. Their armor is their civilization's craft — stone and something that is not quite metal, worked in ways the current world cannot replicate, fitted to bodies the current world does not have.

They wear masks. Not the Barong mask — older than that, the template from which the Barong mask tradition eventually descended. Heavy, ceremonial, the iconography belonging to a religion that predated Devata's by millennia. The mask faces are solemn. They have been solemn for a very long time.

When Natha enters Pura Adi wearing the Barong mask, the remnants see something that resembles the iconography of their master. Not identical — centuries of religious evolution separate Natha's mask from theirs. But close enough to register. Whatever that recognition does to them, it does not make them stand down.

---

## Regions

Pura Adi only.

---

## Combat

Skilled. Deliberate. They fight with the technique of warriors trained in a tradition that no longer exists anywhere outside this threshold — forms and patterns that Natha has never encountered, that no living teacher could have taught him. It is readable as combat, as technique, as something trained and practiced. What it is specifically is unknown.

They use weapons of their civilization's make — heavy, balanced differently than anything from the current era. The forms they fight with were designed for these weapons and these bodies.

**Attacks:**
- **Ancient Strike** — a deliberate, powerful blow. Single hit, significant reach. The timing is slightly off from anything Natha has fought — the rhythm of a different combat tradition. Parry hitbox on windup.
- **Combination** — two-hit sequence from their tradition. The second hit comes from an unexpected angle — not the angle a current-era fighter would use. Parry hitbox on first hit.
- **Shield Press** — advances with a heavy shield, forcing Natha back. Not damaging on its own. Creates space for a follow strike. Parry hitbox on the follow strike.
- **Ancient Form** — a specific stance that signals a sequence of three attacks in their tradition's style. Readable in hindsight, not on first encounter. All three hits have parry hitboxes.

**Parry Hitbox:** Ancient Strike, first hit of Combination, follow strike after Shield Press, all hits of Ancient Form.

---

## When They Disappear

When the Guardian falls, the remnants vanish simultaneously — present one moment, absent the next, cleanly and completely. No bodies. No remains. The threshold releases them and they return to wherever their civilization went.

The masks are gone with them. Natha never gets to hold one. Whatever they were, they belonged to something that is now finished.

---

## Notes

The Ancient Remnants are the only enemies in the game with no connection to Rangda, the occupation, or the current world's conflict. They are entirely separate — a different story, a different age, preserved in amber by a cosmic accident and finally concluded by the arrival of the one person wearing the right face.

Fighting them is fighting something that has no malice toward Natha specifically. It is not personal. It is the duty, and the duty requires the challenge regardless of who is on the other side.

The masks they wear are the oldest version of what Natha's mask eventually became. This is not stated anywhere in the game. It is available to anyone who looks at both and thinks about what they are looking at.
