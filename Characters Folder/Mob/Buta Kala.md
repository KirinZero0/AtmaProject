# Buta Kala

### Mob Document | Spirit World Being — Middle Ground

---

## Overview

Lower-realm spirit beings of Balinese cosmology — not malicious by nature, but requiring ritual containment. The caru ceremonies, the offerings at the crossroads shrines, the precise liturgical practice of keeping the lower realms appeased — all of it existed for a reason. The Buta Kala were fed, acknowledged, kept in their proper place within the cosmological order.

The occupation stopped the ceremonies. Nobody is performing caru anymore. The offerings at the Middle Ground's shrines have gone untended for years.

The Buta Kala noticed.

They are not militarized like the Spirit Beast Dogs. Rangda's disciples did not need to weaponize them — the broken rituals did that on their own. They are simply loose, uncontained, doing what they do without anyone to manage it.

---

## Appearance

Small — goblin-scale, crouching posture that makes them look smaller still. Dark skin, almost black, the color of the lower realm they came from. Bulging eyes. Wide fanged mouth. Wild hair. The shape is recognizable from temple carvings — the Kala face above doorways, the demonic figures in the corner of every traditional Balinese stonework, now given legs and appetite.

They look exactly like what they are. Balinese religious architecture has been depicting them accurately for centuries. Nobody expected to see them walking around.

---

## Regions

Middle Ground — near shrines, crossroads, offering sites. Anywhere the ceremonies used to be performed and are no longer.

---

## Behavior (Non-Combat)

Found crouching at shrine areas, eating offerings — fruit, rice, flowers, whatever was left at the base of a statue and never replaced. They do not scatter when Natha approaches. They look up. They assess. If Natha moves toward the shrine, they treat it as competition for the food.

This is the first encounter in most shrine areas — not a patrol, not a guard. Just something that has been eating undisturbed and does not intend to stop.

---

## Combat

Pack-based. Individually weak, collectively chaotic — they do not coordinate, they just all attack at once from different angles, which produces a similar effect. Fast, low to the ground, erratic movement that does not follow a readable pattern.

**Attacks:**
- **Scratch** — fast claw swipe. Low damage, high frequency. Primary attack.
- **Bite** — lunge and bite. More damage than Scratch, shorter range.
- **Swarm** — when three or more Buta Kala attack simultaneously, they briefly overwhelm Natha's stagger recovery — hits land before he can reset. Not a designed coordination, just what happens when enough of them attack at once.
- **Shriek** — a loud call when one Buta Kala spots Natha. Alerts all nearby Buta Kala in the area. Not damaging. The moment before everything gets worse.

**Parry Hitbox:** None. Erratic movement and no trained technique — nothing generates a parry window.

---

## Notes

Killing Buta Kala is not liberation in the spirit beast sense — they are not stranded beings built a corrupted body by the land. They crossed over because the ritual boundary failed. Destroying the material form sends them back to the lower realm they came from. Whether that is mercy or just displacement is a theological question the game does not answer.

The offering plates they were eating from are still at the shrine when Natha arrives. The fruit is half-eaten. The flowers are scattered. The statue they were sitting in front of still has its face.

Small detail. Available to anyone who looks.
