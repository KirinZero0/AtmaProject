# Elephant Commander

### Mob Document | Mini-Boss — Capital Outskirts

---

## Overview

A senior commander in Rangda's military, stationed in the Capital Outskirts as the visible enforcement of the lockdown. Not one of the Twin Gatekeepers — this is the officer in the field, the face of the occupation's ground presence before Natha reaches the gate plaza. He rides an enhanced war elephant, and the combination of the two is the encounter.

He is unnamed. He is competent. He was placed here because the Outskirts needed something that could not be ignored, and a commander on an enhanced elephant cannot be ignored.

---

## Appearance

**The Commander**

Senior officer markings — heavier rank insignia than a standard Commander, the armor of someone who has held this position long enough to personalize it. Carries a long spear suited for use from horseback — or in this case, from the back of something considerably larger than a horse. Controlled. Does not look concerned.

**The Elephant**

Large — enhanced with spirit beast cellular material, the program applied to an animal rather than a human subject for the first time at this scale. The enhancement did not change the shape: it is still recognizably an elephant. What it changed was everything underneath the shape — the aggression, the durability, the threshold for what registers as a threat. Armor plating has been fitted across the head, shoulders, and flanks. The eyes are wrong in the way enhanced beings' eyes tend to be wrong.

---

## Region

Capital Outskirts — mid-area encounter before the Capital Gate Plaza.

---

## Phase One: Mounted

The Commander directs the elephant from above while attacking with his spear at elevated range. The elephant is the primary threat — large enough to occupy significant space, durable enough to take hits without stopping, aggressive enough to not give Natha room to focus on the rider.

**Elephant Attacks:**
- **Charge** — straight-line rush across the area. Massive damage on contact. Telegraphed by a brief backward step before committing.
- **Stomp** — raises one foreleg and brings it down. Ground shockwave in close range.
- **Tusk Sweep** — wide horizontal arc with tusks. Covers both sides simultaneously.
- **Trample** — if Natha is knocked down, the elephant moves over his position. Continuous damage while contact persists.

**Commander Attacks (from mount):**
- **Spear Thrust** — downward stab from elevation. Long reach, hard to dodge laterally. Parry hitbox on windup.
- **Spear Sweep** — horizontal arc at elevated range, covering the space around the elephant's head. Parry hitbox on windup.

**Phase Condition:** Elephant must be defeated. The Commander cannot be meaningfully targeted while mounted — he is too high and too mobile on the elephant's back.

---

## Phase Two: Dismounted

The elephant falls. The Commander lands — controlled, not thrown. He takes a moment. Looks at the elephant. Looks at Natha.

Then he fights.

On foot he is fast and precise — a senior officer who did not reach this rank by riding. The spear handles differently without elevation but he has been using it long enough that the adjustment is immediate. No rage, no grief visible. He processes what happened and moves on. The job is still the job.

**Commander Attacks (dismounted):**
- **Spear Combo** — two-hit thrust sequence, fast. Parry hitbox on first hit.
- **Spinning Strike** — full rotation with the spear, wide arc. Parry hitbox on windup.
- **Lunge** — explosive forward thrust, covers distance fast. Parry hitbox on extension.
- **Deflect** — raises the spear to intercept Natha's attack. Not a parry hitbox — this is the Commander actively deflecting. If Natha attacks into it, Natha is the one staggered.

**When he falls:**

He goes down without ceremony. No last words. The lockdown continues regardless — the soldiers at their posts do not break formation because their field commander fell. But the largest visible symbol of the occupation's enforcement is on the ground, and the path deeper into the Outskirts is open.

---

## Notes

The elephant is the statement. Rangda wanted the Outskirts locked down in a way that communicated scale — not just soldiers at posts but something that announced that what was coming to enforce this order was not ordinary military presence. An enhanced war elephant in the middle of a civilian settlement is that announcement.

The Commander on foot in Phase Two is the reminder that the program produces dangerous individuals, not just dangerous animals. He is harder to fight dismounted than most of what Natha has faced in the Outskirts.
