# Tiger Guard

### Mob Document | Rangda's Infantry — Spirit Tiger Enhanced

---

## Overview

Soldiers enhanced with spirit tiger cellular material — the refinement program's answer to speed. Where Rakshasa enhancement produced soldiers built for force, tiger enhancement produced soldiers built for closing distance and overwhelming before a response is possible. Light, fast, aggressive. A completely different problem than a Mutated Commander.

---

## Appearance

Noticeably lighter than standard infantry — the enhancement stripped down their armor deliberately, trading protection for mobility. What remains sits close to the body: fitted plate at the chest and shoulders, little else. The frame underneath is not visibly enlarged the way Rakshasa-enhanced soldiers are — the tiger cellular material did not add bulk, it added something that reads in movement rather than size. The way they hold themselves before attacking is wrong — coiled, weight forward, not the posture of a trained soldier but something closer to a predator about to move.

Faint striping is visible on exposed skin. Not uniform. Not decorative. The cellular material leaving its mark.

---

## Regions

Cliff Temple, Capital Outskirts, Narendra. Appears alongside standard infantry as a fast flanker in formed groups.

---

## Combat

Burst-based. Not continuous pressure — explosive short-range aggression that closes distance faster than expected, delivers several hits, then resets. The rhythm is harder to read than a footsoldier's because the pause before each burst varies. Waiting for the pattern gets Natha hit.

**Attacks:**
- **Burst Dash** — explosive forward lunge covering significant distance almost instantly. Followed immediately by a strike on arrival. Parry hitbox active on the strike frame, not the dash.
- **Flurry** — rapid multi-hit combo at close range. Three to four hits in quick succession. Parry hitbox on the first hit only — the rest are too fast to individually window.
- **Feint** — begins a Burst Dash animation and cancels into a different direction. Used to bait dodges. No damage. No parry hitbox.
- **Pounce** — leaps onto Natha from slightly above if terrain allows. Knockdown on hit. Parry hitbox on the descent frame.

**Parry Hitbox:** Burst Dash strike, Flurry first hit, Pounce descent.

---

## Notes

Tiger Guards are highest priority in mixed groups — their speed means they reach Natha before slower infantry and disrupt positioning before a fight settles. Dealing with them first makes the rest of the encounter manageable. Ignoring them does not.

The feint is the tell that distinguishes experienced Tiger Guards from freshly deployed ones. Early appearances have fewer feints. Later appearances use them more freely.
