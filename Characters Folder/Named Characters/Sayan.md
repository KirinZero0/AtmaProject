# Sayan

**The Wandering Witch Hunter**

Relation: [[Prince Natha]], [[Wikananda]]
## Overview

Sayan is a wandering witch hunter from the north, born into a noble family whose name he no
longer uses — a displaced noble who left his homeland after his family deemed him unworthy.
He travels Southeast Asia alone, hunting supernatural threats, building a reputation nobody at
home will ever hear about. He arrived in Natha's kingdom before the prince, drawn by rumors of
a witch queen ruling a decaying land. He came for glory. He stayed because the job isn't finished.

He is not a hero. He is a professional — or at least, a man who has convinced himself that is what
he is.

## Background

Sayan was born into a noble family with expectations he could never satisfy in the way they
demanded. Rather than continue failing in a place that had already decided what he was, he left.
No farewell. No explanation. Just gone.

What followed were years on the road — moving between kingdoms, hunting things that
frightened people who had nowhere else to turn. Each threat faced alone. Each survival a quiet
proof of something he never stops needing to prove.

He earns Sak Yant tattoos along the way, each one given by a monk or spiritual master after
surviving something that should have killed him. They are not decoration. They are a record. A
body that tells the story of everywhere he has been and everything he has endured, written in
sacred geometry across his skin.

He hunts Rangda not because someone hired him. Because he heard there was a witch queen
bringing down an entire kingdom — and that is the kind of thing that makes a name. Something
undeniable. Something even his family could not look away from.

He has not gone home. He tells himself he will, after this.

## Personality

Sayan is calm in a way that takes practice to achieve. Not naturally detached — deliberately so.
He has learned that composure is its own form of armor, and he wears it as carefully as anything
else he carries.

He is intelligent and observant, the kind of person who understands a situation before he
appears to engage with it. He shares information selectively — not out of malice, but because


knowledge is the only leverage a lone wanderer reliably has. He tells enough to be useful. He
keeps the rest.

He is not cruel. He is not cold. He simply does not let people in easily, and when he does, he does
not acknowledge that he has done it.

He uses his appearance deliberately. He knows the effect it has. He lets it work for him and calls it
practicality.

Underneath all of it is someone who has never stopped wanting to be seen — not as a weapon,
not as a curiosity, but as someone worth knowing. He would never say this. The mask covers the
part of his face that might.

## Appearance

Sayan is striking in a way that disarms before he intends it to. Long dark hair, worn loose. A face
that reads as open and approachable until you look long enough to notice it isn't.

He is shirtless, his torso and arms covered extensively in **Sak Yant** — traditional Thai sacred
tattoos. Dense geometric patterns, Khom script, yantra symbols across his chest, shoulders, collar
bones, and arms. Every line earned. Every tattoo a chapter. The coverage is significant enough
that at a distance his skin reads as textured rather than bare.

He wears **loose dark wrap pants** , practical for movement, slightly worn at the edges from years
of travel. A fabric sash tied at the waist holds small pouches and wrapped supplies. He carries
only what he needs and nothing he doesn't.

Across his face he wears the **lower half of a Khon mask** — the ceremonial Thai performance
mask tradition, covering from the nose down. The mask is ornate, white with gold detailing, teeth
visible in the stylized jaw. It obscures his mouth, his expressions, his origin. Above it his eyes do
all the work.

He carries **twin blades** — curved, worn at his hips. Functional, not ceremonial. The handles worn
from use. On his back he carries a **compact composite bow** , the weapon of patience and
distance, a witch hunter's first instinct before committing to anything closer.

## The Mask

The Khon mask is not armor. It is not ritual. It is a choice he makes every morning and unmakes
for no one.

It covers the bottom half of his face — the part that would have to explain where he came from,
who his family is, why he left. The part that might say something he hasn't decided to say yet.

He takes it off once, for Prince Natha, after their second encounter resolves. No explanation. Just
removes it. Shows his face. Puts it back on.


It is the closest he comes to saying: _I think you are real. I think you are worth knowing._

## The Sak Yant

Each tattoo has a name. Each has a story. Together they form a map of everywhere Sayan has
been and everything he has survived — a record written on his body because he has no one to tell
it to.

The tattoos are believed to grant protection against supernatural interference. They are the
reason corruption does not touch him the way it touches others in the decaying kingdom. They
are the reason he has survived this long doing what he does.

They are not enough against a witch queen. He knows this. He came anyway.

## Combat

Sayan fights with the economy of someone who has done this alone for years. No wasted
movement. No performance. He opens with the bow — assessing, softening, never engaging
what he doesn't understand at close range yet. When he commits, he commits fully, with a
precision that took a long time to earn.

He is peak human. That is his ceiling and it is genuinely formidable. Against the things he has
hunted across Southeast Asia, it has always been enough.

Rangda is not those things.

_"He carried twin blades and a bow and a face nobody ever fully saw. He said he was there for the
witch queen. He was there for something older than that — a version of himself worth returning
to."_
