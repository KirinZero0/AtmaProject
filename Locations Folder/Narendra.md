# The Capital — Nalendra

**Area Design Document | Balinese Horror RPG**

## Overview

Nalendra — _The Great Temple of the Gods_ — is the original seat of King Dharma's kingdom. Rangda
did not destroy it when she took it. She wore it. The name stayed. The streets stayed. The ceremonies
stayed. Two hundred years of occupation have not rebuilt this city — they have _redirected_ it, slowly
and deliberately, until the original and the corrupted are indistinguishable to anyone born after the
fall.

The capital is the final area of the game. Natha does not walk these streets until everything before has
already happened. He arrives with force.

## Geography & Layout

The city is built on an elevated inland hill, surrounded by jungle and descending rice terraces. From
inside the city, the treeline is visible at every edge — the jungle pressing in, held back by two centuries
of maintained perimeter. The terraces step downward in every direction, beautiful and deliberate,
because Rangda's occupation runs on the appearance of order.

The city is structured on traditional Balinese cosmological logic: sacred goes up, mundane goes down,
impure to the edges. Three layers, terraced into the hillside.

**Four ritual gates** mark the cardinal directions — standard cosmological architecture. Each has been
corrupted differently over two hundred years of occupation. By the time Natha arrives, at least one is
already broken open by the chaos he has caused across the island. The others he fights through.

## Visual Identity

**Ritual everywhere.** Shrines on every surface. Offerings burning at every corner. Incense so
constant the air has color by the time the player reaches the sanctum. This is what the façade was
protecting — the real face of Rangda's occupation expressed as _devotion_ , not decay.

**The corruption is not visible as rot.** It is visible as devotion gone wrong. Offerings too fresh, too
perfectly arranged. Incense burning at midnight when no ceremony calls for it. Priests performing the
right gestures in the wrong order. Nobody notices because nobody remembers the right order
anymore.

**The Statues**

Barong statues stand at every major shrine intersection throughout the city. Rangda kept them. She


did not deface them, did not tear them down. She placed her own statues _alongside_ them — same
scale, flanking buildings, presented as equals — and let two hundred years do the rest.

Visitors do not see occupation. They see a city where the divine order expanded. That was always the
point.

The Barong statues further into the sanctum have subtly shifted expressions. The further in you go,
the more they look like they are grimacing rather than roaring. Nobody changed them. They have just
been standing next to her for two hundred years.

**The headless king statue** is the one crack in the façade. The king's statue — decapitated, standing
in its original ceremonial position, still holding what it was carved holding, still facing its original
direction — appears at multiple scales across all three districts. The head was not left nearby. It was
never left anywhere. Rangda kept the body standing and removed only the head.

The kingdom is still here. The king is not. That is the message. Every person who passes it receives it
without needing it explained.

A life-size version stands inside the throne room itself, directly to the left of the throne, positioned as
if attending court. It has stood there for two hundred years. That one is not for the city. That one is for
her.

## District Breakdown

**I. The Lower City**

_"The City Remembers"_

The outermost layer. Where everyone who is not the puppet court lives — still functioning, still
populated, the wrongness more visible here because the resources to maintain appearances are
thinner. Shrines overcrowded. Offerings slightly off, people substituting what they cannot afford.
Children know not to be out after the evening ceremony ends.

**Barong statues:** Weathered, offerings accumulated at bases over decades. Rangda's statues smaller
here, newer, placed beside rather than replacing. The headless king at civic scale — present on main
streets, unremarked, part of the furniture of daily life.

**Natha's entry:** He arrives with the conspirator force at dawn or near it. The façade is still technically intact
— market setting up, priests at morning shrines, puppet court soldiers on routine patrol. the conspirators
move through the streets and the city _feels_ it before it sees it.

The beat here is not combat-first. It is conversion. Wikananda and the conspirators work the population
while Natha pushes toward the court gate. The player hears it happening behind them — arguments,
then silence, then footsteps joining. By the time Natha reaches the gate, the lower city is behind him.

**The Gate Mechanism:** The court district gate is a ritual lock — four shrines surrounding it, each
corrupted, each requiring reconsecration or forced breaking. Two methods, same result, different


visual. Restoration route: Natha realigns the offerings and reperforms the consecration from memory.
Demolition route: Natha breaks each shrine with the mask's power. The gate opens either way. The
cutscene reflects the method.

**The NPC:** Somewhere in the lower city chaos, findable if the player is paying attention, is an old
woman at a stall that has not moved in forty years. She is not flagged. She watches everything around
her shift without moving herself.

She says the ground beneath a specific alley sounds different when it rains. Her grandmother told her
never to leave offerings at a certain wall because something down there takes them. She does not say
this as exposition. She says it sideways, the way people talk about things they have normalized but not
explained.

She only speaks if Natha approaches her first.

**II. The Court District**

_"The Ones Who Stayed"_

The middle layer. Where the puppet court operates — officials, administrators, Rangda's human
collaborators. Ornate buildings, maintained streets, the largest and most formal statue pairings in the
city. The headless king here is the most prominent version: the original royal monument, centrally
positioned, unavoidable. The cut at the neck still visible if examined closely.

**On Natha's arrival:** The puppet court did not flee. Some are true believers. Some have been
collaborating for so long they have nowhere else to be. They fight — not well, but with the desperation
of people who made a choice two hundred years of family history ago and cannot unmake it. The
conspirators handle most of this. Background combat is at its densest here.

**The City Center:** Wide, ceremonially paved, lined with Barong statues. Open space in the middle of
the district — the civic heart of the original kingdom, still maintained as such.

Natha pushes through toward the far end. Then:

A massive ornamental spear crashes into the city center paving in front of him. No warning. It hits
with enough force to crack the stone outward from the impact point. The vibration travels. Natha
stops not because he decides to but because his body decides for him.

Beat.

Ki Luruh drops from a building elevation, lands beside the spear, pulls it free in the same motion she
uses to settle into her stance.

No words. Fight begins.

_The crack in the paving remains for the rest of the game. The spear is gone. Just the damage._

**Boss: Ki Luruh — Second Form**


No half-mask. Real face fully exposed — both halves, entirely hers. Armor stripped to functional
minimum. Hair loose. The cracks across her skin where death left marks that Rangda's reconstruction
sealed but could not fully erase suggest light from underneath, the seams visible the way repaired
ceramic shows its joinery.

She is not here because Rangda sent her. She chose to be here. That distinction is the whole tragedy.

This fight is quieter than the first. More final. Her moveset is cleaner — less performance, more
precision. She is not trying to impress anyone. She is finishing something on her own terms, in
defense of the woman who never fully valued her, because that is who she decided to be.

**When she falls:** No death cutscene. She goes down, stays down. Natha burns her soul before moving on — not her body, her soul, permanent death — and continues without stopping. She does not appear again.

**III. The Sanctum Approach**

_"What Devotion Looks Like at the End"_

The innermost district before the palace. The ritual architecture here is dense enough to feel
suffocating — shrines on every surface, offerings burning continuously, the Barong statues lining the
approach path grimacing in their stone. The air has color. This is the concentrated face of everything
the lower city was papering over.

the conspirator force does not reach this far. From here, Natha is alone.

**The Inner Court:** Rangda's ritual practitioners — not soldiers. People who kept the corruption
liturgically maintained for two centuries. They fight differently, more coordinated, less aggressive, like
they are performing a ceremony that happens to be lethal.

**The Garden:** Off the main corridor. Not hidden, not locked — just a turn the player does not have to
take. No marker. A ceremonial courtyard open to the sky, overgrown at the edges in a way the rest of
the sanctum is not. The offerings here are genuinely old, dried out, untended. In the center, Antara
sits on the base of a broken shrine, one leg folded, completely still. A spirit-world beast half-visible
beside him — present in a way things from that side should not be. He pets it with the familiarity of
something known.

He does not startle when Natha enters. He knew.

**Antara's dialogue — by corruption state:**

_Low corruption (Dominated path):_

```
"You're still in there. I wasn't certain you would be, this far in."
"She's waiting for you. Not the mask. You. I think she's forgotten what that feels like — someone
coming for her rather than running from her."
"Don't waste it."
```
_High corruption (Consumed path):_

```
"There it is."
```

```
"I've watched this happen before. Different islands. Different contracts. The vessel always thinks
it's still choosing."
"Are you? Still choosing?"
"Go finish it."
```
_Mid corruption (Sealed path):_

```
"You know what I came here to see? The moment it tips. One way or the other."
"You haven't tipped yet. This close to the end."
"Remarkable. And completely useless, probably."
```
He does not wait for response. He is done when he is done. The garden is missable. Antara does not
chase the player down.

**The Corridor:** The main path to the palace entrance. Long and deliberate. Every shrine active, every
offering burning. The sound of the conspiracy has completely disappeared. Just Natha's footsteps and
the incense.

Then Sayan. Standing in the threshold at the far end. The Rangda mask pressed against his face — not
his Khon mask, _Rangda's_ iconography on the face of the man who wore a mask by choice. The Sak
Yant tattoos that should protect him are cracked through, dark corruption running in the fracture
lines.

His stance is his. His eyes are not.

His monologue begins before Natha reaches him. He does not turn. He speaks forward, into the
palace entrance, as if addressing something inside:

```
"I came here for a name. Something worth carrying back. Something that would make the
distance make sense."
"I found it. Just not the way I thought."
```
He turns. The mask catches light from the corridor shrines.

```
"You should have left the island when you still knew how."
```
Fight begins.

**Boss: Sayan — Controlled**

He fights exactly like himself. Twin blades, composite bow, the patience and economy of a man who
hunts alone. Every technique he earned, every scar the Sak Yant covers, now pointed at the one
person on the island he chose to show his face to. Rangda did not remake him. She redirected him.

The fight feels wrong in a specific way. Like fighting someone who is not trying to kill you but the
thing wearing them is.

**When he falls:** The mask cracks — not shatters, cracks. Sayan drops. The corruption recedes enough
that he is present for a moment. Eyes his own. Body wrecked. He understands what happened without
needing it explained.


_Dominated:_ Natha catches him before he hits the ground. He performs a purification — not a
dramatic ritual, something quieter, the mask's power used for the first and maybe only time to give
rather than take. The corruption in the cracked Sak Yant lines recedes. Sayan survives. In the ending
crowd scene he is there, held up by two conspirators on either side of him, present but not standing on his
own. He does not kneel. He probably cannot. But he is himself and he is there.

_Consumed:_ Natha does not stop. The thing wearing him registers Sayan as an obstacle that has
stopped moving and nothing more. The kill is immediate, without pause, without acknowledgment.
No moment of recognition passes between them. The brutality is not personal — which is the worst
thing about it. Sayan spent his life hunting things that deserved to be hunted. He ends as collateral.

_Sealed:_ Natha catches him before he hits the ground — that much happens. Then he sets him down
against the corridor wall and straightens up. Sayan looks at him, fully present, fully aware of what the
corruption still running through his cracked Sak Yant lines means for him. He asks Natha to finish it.
Not dramatically. He begs for it, from the floor, broken, with the clarity of a man who has thought
about what it means to be used as a weapon against someone he chose and arrived at a very clear
conclusion about what should come next.

Natha walks past him toward the throne room.

He heard it. He understood it. He does not have enough left to answer it.

Sayan is alive at the end of the Sealed path — dying slowly on the corridor floor, the corruption
progressing through the fractures in his tattoos, the protection that was supposed to keep him safe
now keeping him conscious through all of it. Nobody comes back down the corridor. the conspirators do not
reach this far. He is alone with it. The Sealed ending does not show what happens to him. It does not
need to.

**IV. Rangda's Throne Room**

_"The End of Everything"_

The original royal audience hall — enormous, high-ceilinged, stone columns running the full length.
Built to make everyone who enters feel appropriately small. Rangda did not redecorate. She sat down
and let two hundred years of her presence do the work.

The corruption here is not theatrical. It is _settled_. Offerings layered centuries deep — the newest on
top of the oldest, calcified into sediment. The torches burn a color that is almost correct. The air is
completely still.

The headless king stands to the left of the throne. Life-size. Attending court. As it has for two hundred
years.

Rangda is seated on the throne at the far end. Composed. She watches Natha cross the entire length of
the room. She lets him walk the whole way.

She has time.


**Phase One — The Doll Form**

The figure on the throne reads, at first glance, as Rangda. The right silhouette, the right iconography.
The wrongness becomes clear when the fight begins — movements slightly mechanical, joints bending
with precision rather than weight, a face correct in every detail except expression.

Four identical puppets stand at the cardinal points of the room. Same height, same blank face, same
posture. They activate with the doll, responding to the same signal, extensions of the same will.

**The doll conducts. It does not engage directly.** The puppets move in patterns dictated by the
doll's gestures — Natha must track both simultaneously. Attack a puppet while the doll gestures and
the other three respond. Focus on the doll and the puppets swarm. Damaged puppets go dormant
temporarily; the doll reconstitutes them. The only way to end the phase is to damage the doll itself.

As the doll takes damage the puppets become erratic — control signal degrading, patterns less
coordinated, more aggressive. By the threshold they are interfering with each other's movements.

The doll goes still. Mid-gesture. Just stops.

The four puppets collapse simultaneously.

Silence. The throne room holds it.

Then Rangda stands up from behind the throne. She was seated on the floor back there — not hiding,
just present, separate from the performance, waiting for it to finish. She rises slowly. The doll on the
throne is already irrelevant. It was always irrelevant.

She looks at Natha across the length of the room.

**Pre-fight dialogue — by corruption state:**

_Low/Mid corruption (Dominated/Sealed):_

```
"You're still wearing it."
```
Not an accusation. An observation.

```
"Every king before you — the mask took them before they reached this room. Your grandfather.
His father. His father's father. Eight hundred years of bloodline burning through that wood and
none of them made it here with anything left."
```
She looks at the mask specifically.

```
"Barong chose well. I'll give him that much."
```
She stands. The motion unhurried.

```
"I killed my daughter."
```
Not for sympathy. Because it is the truest thing in the room and she is tired of it living only inside her.


```
"Not by accident. Not in the chaos. I made a choice and she was standing in the wrong place and I
made it anyway."
"A thousand years and I still don't know what to do with that. So."
```
She raises her hands. The room responds.

```
"Let's finish this."
```
_High corruption (Consumed):_

She looks at Natha. Then at the mask. Then back.

```
"...He's not in there anymore, is he."
```
Not a question. Something behind her expression shifts — something that might be disappointment if
she still had capacity for it.

She stands. No further words. Fight begins.

The silence is the dialogue. She had things to say to the prince. The thing wearing him does not
warrant them.

**Phase Two — The Real Rangda**

Everything changes. The throne room does not feel smaller — it feels _occupied_ differently. The doll
controlled the space from the throne. Rangda fills it.

Her monstrous form is the Rangda of the mythology carrying two hundred years of accumulated grief
and fury and the specific weight of what she did on her daughter's wedding night. Long white hair that
moves without wind. The iconography that belongs to her — the bulging eyes, the fangs, the hands
that register wrong before the player can articulate why. She does not perform any of this. It is just
what she is now.

**The room responds to her.** The corruption in the walls, the centuries of ritual sediment — she
draws on all of it. The environment becomes part of her moveset.

On the Consumed route she is more aggressive, more efficient. The dialogue did not happen. There is
nothing to resolve. She is just finishing it.

On Dominated/Sealed routes something underneath the fury is almost _tired_. Not holding back. Just
carrying weight.

## Endings — The Throne Room

**Dominated**

Rangda falls. The fire that burns through her does not destroy dramatically — it _returns_. Whatever is


underneath a thousand years of grief burns away until what remains is the original thing: a frail
human woman, finally finished. She turns to ash not because she is destroyed but because without the
power there is nothing holding the body together anymore. She outlived herself by centuries. The
ending makes that visible.

Natha stands. He reaches up slowly and takes the mask off. Not a struggle — a choice. He sets it on the
armrest of the throne beside him.

_If the player found Widiasta's chamber and carries Ratna Manggali's wedding jewelry:_ Natha sets
it in the ash. No cutscene explaining why. Just the object, placed. The camera holds on it.

The people file in — conspirators first, then civilians, then whoever survived. They kneel unevenly,
organically, as they arrive. It does not look like ceremony. It looks like relief.

The camera finds Antara in the crowd. Not kneeling. Standing near the back, watching Natha on the
throne with an expression that is genuinely difficult to read. He came to see the architecture collapse.
He watched someone refuse to be machinery. He does not know what to do with that.

He catches Natha's eye across the crowd — or Natha catches his. Nothing passes between them. Then
Antara looks away first.

When the camera pulls to the wide shot — full room, light through high windows, the headless king
still standing in the corner — Antara is gone. The space where he was is just crowd.

He got what he came for. Something different than what he expected. He left.

**Consumed**

The throne room is empty.

Not cleared — _emptied_. Whatever happened in here when the mask finished, people felt it from
outside and did not come in. The doors are open. Nobody crossed the threshold.

Natha on the throne. Barong mask still on, hollow eyes catching no light. His posture settled — not
slumped, not rigid. Settled, the way something settles when it has found exactly where it belongs and
stopped moving. The throne fits him perfectly. It was always going to.

Then the player notices the figure near the throne.

Antara. Standing just inside the edge of the throne's shadow. Not hiding — just occupying the space
the way he occupies every space, slightly outside the rules of it. He is not looking at Natha. He is
looking at the mask.

His expression is the one from the garden — genuinely curious, clinically interested. But underneath it
something quieter that was not in the garden. Not grief. The specific feeling of being right about
something you wanted to be wrong about.

He does not speak. Natha does not move.

The camera holds.


Fade.

**Sealed**

Natha walks to the throne.

He is tired in the way that has nothing theatrical in it — a young man who has been carrying
something enormous and has finally run out of the thing that kept him moving. He reaches the throne
because it is the furthest point in the room and sitting down is the only logical next action.

He sits.

He is unconscious before he can decide what comes after.

The camera holds on him, alone, in the empty room. No crowd.

Then, at the far end of the room — Antara. Not materialized. No fog, no sound. Just present in the way he is always present, watching from the threshold with an expression that does not resolve into anything readable. He watches for a long moment.

Then he is simply not there.

Fade.

## Hidden Area — Widiasta's Chamber

Accessible from the palace underbelly. Not on the main path. Not marked.

**Discovery:** The old woman in the lower city. Found only if the player approaches her during the
conversion sequence. She says the ground beneath a specific alley sounds different when it rains. Her
grandmother told her never to leave offerings at a certain wall.

The entrance is in the lower city — a section of wall that opens rather than just standing there. Old
ritual architecture repurposed as a prison. The chains holding Widiasta are not iron. They are
inscribed — cosmological language, the same logic as the mask contract. Rangda did not need bars.
She needed _language_.

**Widiasta:** A thousand years chained, unable to die, in the dark beneath the city of the woman who
killed the person he loved. The immortality has kept him present without keeping him intact. He
speaks in fragments — correct theology, accurate memory, real words — but the sequence is gone. He
tells Natha something true and devastating and then says something that sounds like a child's prayer
and then goes silent and then picks up mid-sentence from something he was saying four hundred
years ago.

He does not know how long it has been. He thinks it has been a long time.

**The reward:** A piece of Ratna Manggali's wedding jewelry. Something from that night that ended up
in his hands and never left. In the Dominated ending, Natha places it in Rangda's ash. No cutscene.
No dialogue. Just the object returned to where it was always going.

**Ending branches:**

```
Dominated: Natha breaks the chains. Widiasta does not thank him. He sits in the absence of the
chains and breathes. The epilogue notes in a single line that he walked out of the city at dawn
and nobody saw which direction he went.
```

```
Consumed: Widiasta reaches toward Natha. Natha walks past. The mask does not want him
stopping here.
Sealed: Natha never finds the chamber. Not hidden from him. He just never goes deep enough.
```
**On Rangda and Widiasta:** She could have killed him eventually. She kept him breathing in the
dark for a thousand years because destroying him would mean finishing it. He is the last living
connection to Ratna Manggali. She would never admit this. It may not be fully conscious. But it is
there.
