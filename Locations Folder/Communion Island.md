# Communion Island

### Area Design Document | Balinese Horror RPG

---

## Overview

A small island sitting off the coast of Devata Island, invisible from the water, perpetually shrouded in boundary fog — the kind that forms where the material world and spirit world press against each other long enough that the membrane between them softens.

Nobody goes there. Not Rangda's troops, not the conspirators, not travelers. The fog discourages approach in a way that isn't hostile — just unwelcoming, the way a space feels when it belongs to something else.

The only way in from the material world is through a hidden passage in the conspirator caverns, opened by the audience mantra. Antara doesn't use doors — he travels via spirit world and has been coming here for centuries.

---

## Geography

Small. One ruined temple at the center — ancient, pre-occupation, pre-kingdom. Stone with its own ecosystem: moss, roots through the joints, offerings left by visitors across centuries. Oriented toward something that isn't a cardinal direction, built around a central meditation point rather than a deity's shrine.

The fog sits at the island's edges. Inside the fog line the air is still, clear, and thin — the boundary between worlds is closest here. Spirit beasts wander through casually. Small material world creatures live here permanently: birds, lizards, whatever survives on a small foggy island with no predators and no human interference.

Nothing on this island has ever been threatened. The animals show it. They approach rather than flee.

Antara has been coming here since before the kingdom existed. The offerings in the ruins across different centuries — some of them are his. Small ones. The kind left without liturgical significance. He kept coming back and the place became his in the way places become yours when you've loved them long enough.

---

## The Audience Mantra

### What It Is

Not a spell. A formal address — an introduction of self to a being of cosmological rank. The correct protocol for requesting audience with the king of the spirit world. Done wrong it is meaningless. Done right it opens Barong's palace doors.

Rangda didn't destroy this knowledge during the occupation. She buried it — because a prince who could walk into Barong's palace and confront him directly was the one thing her system couldn't administrate around. The difference between destroying and burying matters. She was afraid of it.

### Finding It

A physical document — a lontar manuscript or stone inscription — in the oldest surviving pre-occupation records. The player assembles the picture of what they need across multiple regions, hunts for the specific text, finds it. It is not handed to Natha. It is earned.

### Wikananda's Reaction

Natha brings the mantra to Wikananda. Shows him.

Wikananda is quiet for a moment.

_"You actually found it."_

Not surprised. Relieved. A contingency plan he never expected to activate just activated.

He teaches Natha the correct recitation — pronunciation, ritual context, what the mantra is formally saying. Then he shows Natha the hidden entrance in the caverns.

He does not mention he has been going there himself for years. He does not mention he always felt watched.

---

## Accessing the Island

The hidden entrance is in the conspirator caverns — looks like a wall until the audience mantra is recited correctly in front of it. Then it opens.

The passage is long enough that arriving on the other side feels like arriving somewhere.

Natha emerges on Communion Island. Antara is already there.

---

## Meeting

_Mid game — after earning the audience mantra. Antara and Natha already know each other by this point._

Antara is sitting in the ruins. A spirit beast nearby. A lizard somewhere on or near him. He is not doing anything in particular.

He looks up. The specific stare of someone who has kept a secret for centuries and is watching it become not a secret anymore.

_"...You."_

_"How did you find this place."_

Not angry. Genuinely asking. Nobody has ever needed to ask before.

Natha explains — the mantra, Wikananda, the passage.

Antara processes this. Looks around the island like he's taking inventory of what's still his.

_"The priest came here sometimes. I never let him see me. He seemed — I didn't want to be part of whatever he was doing."_

_"You're different. The mask. Of course."_

A beat. Something unguarded that none of his other appearances have.

_"I've been coming here since before your kingdom existed. It's the only place I can — "_

He stops. Decides not to finish that sentence.

_"It's remote. No one looks for me here. Usually just me and whatever wandered over from the other side."_

He gestures at the spirit beast. It doesn't react.

_"You're not here to sit, are you."_

Not a question. He already knows.

Natha explains — Barong, the atma corruption, the spirit world.

Antara nods once. Whatever he feels about Natha walking into Barong's palace he keeps to himself.

_"The temple still works. It has for as long as I've been coming here."_

He settles back. Done with the conversation. Not unfriendly — just done.

---

## The Meditation & Crossing

Natha goes to the temple's central meditation point. Sits. Recites the audience mantra — the formal address, the introduction of self, the request for audience.

The fog changes. Not lifts — _changes_. The boundary goes from thin to absent. The ruined temple becomes a threshold. Barong's palace appears on the other side.

On the Dominated path the mask resists. Natha holds the meditation against its pull — a will check, not a fight. The player sits with the discomfort and chooses to stay.

Natha crosses. Antara watches him go. He doesn't follow — Barong's palace is Barong's domain. He sits back down.

He'll find out how it went.

---

## Barong Fight — Failure Dialogues

Each failure expels Natha from the spirit world back onto Communion Island. Antara is always already there. Same spot.

**First failure:**

_"...How did it go."_

Not a question.

_"He's difficult."_

_"...Yes."_

_"Do you want to sit for a moment."_

Practical. Not warm. Natha looks like he needs to sit.

**Second failure:**

Antara says nothing. Moves slightly to make room on the ruins.

The spirit beasts don't look up. They're used to this now.

**Third failure:**

_"You know you can just — "_

_"Don't."_

_"I wasn't going to say give up."_

_"What were you going to say."_

_"...I was going to say sit here for a while first. Before going back."_

Beat.

_"The small ones help. Somehow."_

He gestures vaguely at the animals.

**Fourth failure or beyond:**

Antara silently hands Natha whatever small creature is currently sitting nearest to him.

No words. The creature sits in Natha's hands unbothered.

This is the most comforting thing in the entire game.

**On eventual victory:**

The last time Natha materializes on the island he is not wrecked. He is done.

Antara looks at him and knows immediately without being told.

Whatever he feels about that he keeps to himself. But he keeps it carefully.
