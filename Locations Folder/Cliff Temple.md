# Cliff Temple

### Location Document | Lv 2

---

## Overview

The Cliff Temple was built into the cliff face above the Middle Ground as a place of meditation — a withdrawal from the world, a space for stillness. The king whose statue stands inside its deepest chamber was known as the most patient of the masked kings. The gauntlet he carried was not an aggressive weapon. It was a defensive one. The temple was designed in that image: a place you came to endure, to wait, to become harder to break.

Rangda's disciples found it useful for different reasons.

The layout — labyrinthine, compartmentalized, rooms designed for isolation and extended stay — translated well to a containment facility. The corridors that were meant for solitary passage became corridors for moving specimens. The chambers meant for prolonged meditation became chambers for prolonged confinement. The cliff face location meant the facility was naturally difficult to access and naturally difficult to escape from. It required almost no structural modification.

They added the locks. The rest was already there.

---

## What It Is Now

A research facility. Specifically: a facility attempting to solve a logistics problem.

The distance between the Middle Ground and Narendra is significant. The supply roads take time. Rangda's slave labor in the fields produces efficiently but moves slowly. The disciples identified the bottleneck and proposed a solution — if the transport could be aerial, the logistics chain between the farmland and the capital would be faster, more reliable, and less dependent on road infrastructure that enemy activity could disrupt.

The solution required a subject capable of flight. They found one already here.

Garuda is a Spirit World being — humanoid, bird-natured — that crossed into the material world during a previous cycle and did not return in time. Like any Spirit World being left too long in the material world, it developed a physical body built from the corrupted land around it. By the time the disciples arrived at the Cliff Temple, Garuda was already trapped — already material, already aggressive from the corruption eating through its form, already unable to leave.

They did not capture a free creature. They found one that could not run and built a cage around it.

The disciples began extracting what made Garuda's material body capable of flight and introducing it into human subjects. The goal was a workforce: human enough to follow instructions, capable enough to carry cargo between the terraces and the capital at speed.

Some of the experiments worked. The successful subjects — stable hybrids, functional fliers — were removed from the facility and deployed in Rangda's military logistics operations. They exist. They are working. The proof of concept is established.

The facility's current objective is mass production. The disciples are trying to understand why some subjects stabilize and most do not, and how to make the process reliable enough to scale.

They have not solved this yet.

What they do not understand — what they have no framework to understand — is that what they are replicating is not biology. It is corruption. The material body Garuda developed is built from corrupted land. The flight capability they are extracting and introducing into humans is a property of that corruption. The successful experiments work because the corruption took hold. The failures are simply the ones it destroyed faster.

## The Temple's Irony

The Gauntlet King's statue still stands in the Disciple's Laboratory — the original meditation hall, now a workspace covered in documentation, equipment, and the residue of things that did not survive the process. The statue shows the Barong mask above the gauntlet, the same face that appears on every king's statue across Devata.

The disciples do not look at it. It has been there long enough to become furniture.

The gauntlet represents patience. Defense. The willingness to absorb without breaking. The temple built in its image has been repurposed into a place of systematic impatience — forcing transformation faster than any living thing should be forced to change, breaking the ones that cannot keep up, and calling the survivors a success metric.

When Natha reaches the statue and the mask recognizes itself in stone, the memory that surfaces is quiet. The Gauntlet King did not fight the way the other kings fought. He waited. He let things come to him. He was the hardest king to kill because he was the most difficult to exhaust.

The gauntlet manifests. The irony of receiving it here, in this place, is not lost — or it is, depending on how much Natha is paying attention.

## Enemies

**Shield Soldiers** — Rangda's heavy infantry, stationed throughout the temple. Armor, tower shields, trained specifically for the narrow corridor fighting the labyrinth layout produces. They hold positions. They do not chase far. In the open they are manageable. In the corridors they are walls that hit back.

**Rangda's Disciples** — The experimenters. Not soldiers. They carry tools rather than weapons, and their combat capability reflects that — they are dangerous because of what they can summon or release, not because of their own fighting. They are the ones running the facility. They are also the ones who understand what is in the containment chambers and how to open them under pressure.

**Failed Specimens** — The experiments that did not stabilize. Varying degrees of partial transformation — some with vestigial wings, some with nothing visible on the outside but something clearly wrong on the inside. They wander the sections of the temple that the disciples have written off. Aggressive not from malice but from the specific desperation of something that cannot understand what has been done to it. They cannot be reasoned with. They cannot be cured with what is available in this facility.

**Successful Specimens (caged)** — The ones that worked. Awaiting transport or further assessment. They do not engage Natha if he passes their cells. They watch. The intelligence in what was done to them is still there. They know what this place is.

## Area Breakdown

**I. Cliff Temple Entrance**

The original ceremonial gate, modified. The approach from Temple Summit shows the full facade — cliff-carved stone, old devotional architecture overlaid with the practical additions of an operational facility. Supply lines run in from above. Guards rotate at the entrance.

The Outer Rampart is visible from the entrance — the fortified outer wall with heavy shield soldier presence. It is locked from the inside. Natha cannot force it. The key is elsewhere in the facility.

**II. Outer Rampart (Shield Soldiers)**

The defensive perimeter wall of the temple complex. The shield soldiers here are the densest concentration in the facility — this is the point the disciples would fall back to if the facility was breached from below. They are set up for exactly that scenario.

The gate through the Outer Rampart connects to the Collapsed Hall and Garuda's Containment beyond. Without the key, it does not open.

**III. Research Wing**

The first internal section accessible from the Entrance. The original entry corridor network, now lined with cells on both sides. Documentation posted outside each — subject number, origin, date of introduction, current status. Most statuses are not positive.

The research conducted here is preliminary: intake, initial exposure, early-stage monitoring. The subjects in the Research Wing are at the beginning of the process. Some are still recognizably themselves. This is, in its way, worse than the sections further in.

**IV. Containment Chamber**

Mid-facility. The subjects here are further along — the transformation either stabilizing or failing visibly. The chamber is designed for longer-term holding: more secure locks, thicker walls, cells built to contain things stronger than the person who went in.

Failed specimens have breached some of the cells here. The disciples sealed the affected corridors and continued working in the sections that remained intact. The breached sections are Natha's problem now.

**V. Underground Pass**

A utility tunnel running beneath the main temple structure — service access for the facility's lower sections. Dark, low-ceilinged, not built for combat. Opens a shortcut back to the Entrance for players who find it.

**VI. Central Chamber**

The convergence point of the facility's internal routes. Originally the temple's main meditation hall — high-ceilinged, built to feel open inside the cliff. The disciples use it as an assessment and staging area: successful subjects are processed here before being transported out, documentation is compiled here, decisions about the facility's direction are made here.

It is the most functional-feeling space in the temple. Clean, organized, purposeful. The contrast with the Research Wing and Containment Chamber is deliberate on the disciples' part. This is where things work.

**VII. Disciple's Laboratory**

The primary workspace. The Gauntlet King's statue stands against the far wall — the original meditation hall, repurposed entirely. The stone floor is covered in the apparatus of ongoing research: tools, documentation, materials, containment vessels for Garuda's extracted essence.

The **Gauntlet King Statue** is here. The mask face, the gauntlet in stone hands. Offerings have not been left here in a long time. The disciples have used the offering space as additional counter surface.

The Laboratory connects to the facility's upper section — the Disciple's Laboratory is the highest working area before the exit to the Temple Summit.

**BOSS: Disciple Scientist**

The head of the facility's research operation. Not a combatant by training — a scholar, a specialist, someone who has spent years in this temple pursuing a specific technical problem. She fights with the facility's tools: released specimens, environmental hazards, things from the containment chambers she knows how to open and is willing to open.

She is not panicking. She has contingency plans for facility breach. Natha is a contingency she has planned for.

When she falls, she drops the Outer Rampart key. She has held it because she is the one who decides when the outer gate opens and closes. She understood its importance. She was right.

After the fight, Natha reaches the Gauntlet King Statue. The mask recognizes itself. The memory of the most patient king surfaces. The gauntlet manifests.

**VIII. Collapsed Hall**

The section of the temple that structurally failed — stone ceiling partially down, the floor uneven, the original corridor layout illegible. Garuda's presence has something to do with this. The force required to contain something of Garuda's nature over an extended period has stressed the surrounding structure.

The collapsed section is navigable but not safely. Failed specimens have moved into it — the structural damage made the containment impractical and the disciples abandoned the area.

**IX. Garuda's Containment**

The deepest, most reinforced section of the facility. The disciples built the containment around Garuda after finding it here — the ceiling is high, the walls reinforced, the apparatus layered. It is a cage built by people who understood they were containing something that should not be possible to contain, and built accordingly.

The collapsed hall outside is partially Garuda's work. Not escape attempts — the material body the corruption built is aggressive but not strategic. The structural damage is the product of that aggression over time, the body acting on instinct, the walls absorbing it until some of them stopped absorbing.

What remains of Garuda's original self is still present underneath the corruption. It cannot reach the surface. The material body does not allow it.

**BOSS: Garuda**

The fight is the corruption fighting. The aggression is not Garuda's — it is what the material body does, what it was built to do by the corrupted land it formed from. Garuda cannot stop it. It has been unable to stop it since the body finished forming.

Destroying the material body is the only way through. It is also the only way out — for Garuda.

When the body dissolves, the true form surfaces for a moment. A Spirit World being, bird-natured, finally unweighted from the material that has been crushing it. There is no hostility here. There is something that reads as relief, and then gratitude — the specific gratitude of something that has been suffering for longer than it had words for and has just been given the thing it could not give itself.

Garuda does not leave immediately. There is a moment of recognition — the mask, the being wearing it, what just happened. Then Garuda gives Natha the mantra. The words that summon the Spectral Wings — not a physical object, but a manifestation drawn from what Garuda's true form carries. A gift from something returning to where it belongs, to someone still figuring out where that is.

Then Garuda ascends. The cliff air comes through the damaged containment wall. The cage is empty.

**X. Temple Summit (Wing Required)**

The top of the cliff temple, above the facility. Accessible only with the Spectral Wings. The view from here encompasses the Middle Ground below and the approach to the Capital Outskirts above. 

The summit connects to the Capital Outskirts. The ascent continues.
