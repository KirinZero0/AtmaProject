# Middle Ground

### Location Document | Lv 1

---

## Overview

The Middle Ground is everything between the Shore and the cliffs — the farmland, the forests, the villages, the lake, the old temples half-swallowed by jungle. It is the body of the kingdom. The Shore is the edge that the occupation shows to the sea. Narendra is the seat of power. The Middle Ground is where the people actually lived, and it is where the damage is most visible.

Rangda did not burn the Middle Ground. She uses it. The rice terraces still produce. The trade roads are still maintained. The villages are still inhabited, after a fashion. The corruption here is not the corruption of destruction — it is the corruption of infrastructure. A kingdom repurposed. The same land, the same roads, the same buildings, run by different logic for different ends.

The most effective horror is not the thing that burns everything down. It is the thing that keeps everything running and changes who the labor serves.

---

## Geography & Layout

Rolling terrain. Forest edges and open fields and terraced hillsides descending in stepped platforms toward the coast. The main road runs inland from the Shore's temple gate, splitting at the Crossroads into routes toward the lake, the upper terraces, and eventually the cliff face that marks the end of the low country. Above the terraces, the land rises sharply — the cliff approach, the beginning of the ascent to the Cliff Temple.

The lake sits in a natural basin to the east, ringed by ruins of what was once a significant temple complex. The forest runs along the northern edge of the zone, deeper and denser the further from the road you go. Spirit beasts live in the depths. Rangda's forces do not patrol the forest — they patrol the roads, the fields, the supply lines. The forest is its own problem.

---

## The Corruption Here

The villages in the Middle Ground did not become what they are through direct attack. They became what they are through the food supply.

Rangda's forces introduced spirit beast meat into the food chain — wild animals from the spirit world, hunted and mixed into the supply, indistinguishable from ordinary game to anyone who did not know what they were looking for. The villages ate. The changes were slow at first. Strength, then endurance, then the wrong kind of both. The inability to die is not a gift when it comes paired with the inability to stop.

By the time the villages understood what had happened, it was complete. The people who changed cannot starve. Cannot fatigue. Cannot refuse. They work the terraces now from before dawn to after dark, feeding a capital that imprisoned them. The ones who did not change — who were in the forest when the supply was poisoned, who were traveling, who for whatever reason did not eat from the contaminated stock — are what remains of the human population. They live in camps. They do not live in the villages.

The mutated villagers are not aggressive. They are not monsters in any dramatic sense. They simply work. They follow direction. They do not complain. This is, in certain ways, more disturbing than if they screamed.

## Area Breakdown

**I. Middle Ground Entry**

The gate from the Shore Temple opens here. The road ahead is visible — the first real stretch of open terrain after the coastal layout of the Shore. There is a patrol visible in the middle distance. There is also the beginning of the forest to the north, and the first distant suggestion of the terraces above.

Natha has the Greatsword. He does not yet have the Mystic Sash. The paths that require it are visible but closed.

**II. Forest Edge**

The border between the open road zone and the actual forest. Sparse patrols here — Rangda's forces see the forest as unprofitable territory. Spirit beasts have begun materializing along the tree line, drawn out by the corruption in the land. Not hostile, mostly. Territorial. The distinction matters less the further into the forest you go.

A locked path leads east toward the Lake Approach — the lock will not open until the Lake road below connects it from the other direction.

**III. Crossroads (Corrupted Village 1)**

Where the road branches. The Crossroads is what remains of a farming village — the buildings are intact, the terraces are maintained, the well still functions. The people moving through it are Satya's neighbors and the children of Satya's neighbors, going through the motions of agricultural labor at a pace and regularity that is not human anymore.

There are no conversations to be had here. The corrupted villagers do not speak. They work. They do not acknowledge Natha. They have not acknowledged anyone in a long time.

Rangda's soldiers are posted at intervals — not to supervise the workers, who require no supervision — but to maintain the supply routing. These are soldiers doing administrative work. They are not expecting a fight.

The road to the Upper Road branches north here but is inaccessible — the Mystic Sash is required to cross the terrain feature that blocks it. Natha can see where it goes. He cannot go there yet.

**IV. Open Fields / Lower Road**

The agricultural flatlands between the Crossroads and the lake basin. Open terrain, harder to navigate without being seen. The fields are worked by corrupted laborers. The Lower Road is a maintained supply route — carts, soldier patrols, the regular infrastructure of an occupation that is running efficiently.

Hunter Camp A is at the edge of this area, positioned where the Fields give Satya sightlines without putting him on the road.

**V. Hunter Camp A — Satya**

Satya's camp. Positioned at the border of the Open Fields and the lower terraces — close enough to the Crossroads to watch, far enough that soldiers do not patrol this far off the road.

He has been here for two years. The camp shows it. Not slovenly — a hunter's camp is functional — but lived-in in the way a temporary shelter stops looking temporary after long enough.

He watches Natha arrive. He does not speak first.

When he does speak, the conversation is brief and practical. He knows what Natha is. He knows what the mask can do. He knows where the Overseer is and he will share that information after Natha proves capable of using it. He does not frame this as doubt. He frames it as efficiency.

The **Archer King Statue** stands near the camp — one of several throughout the Middle Ground. The statue shows the Barong mask above the bow, the weapon of the king the forest communities remember most. Natha cannot interact with it yet. The bow has not manifested. That comes later.

**VI. BOSS: The Overseer**

A human collaborator — a noble from Narendra's administrative class who understood agricultural logistics and was willing to apply that understanding in Rangda's service. Not a soldier. Not a monster. A bureaucrat with a guard contingent and a very practical understanding of how to manage a workforce.

He has been posted here long enough to be comfortable. He has stopped thinking about what the workforce is. He thinks in quotas and supply schedules and the efficiency metrics that determine whether this posting remains comfortable or becomes something else.

He fights because he has guards and he gives orders. His actual combat capability is not the point. The point is that he does not believe, until the moment it is too late to matter, that the person who just entered his compound is something the guards cannot handle.

After he falls, Satya is quiet for a moment. Then he accepts the rebel base mantra. Then he packs his camp in under ten minutes and leaves without looking back at the Crossroads.

**VII. Lake Approach / Lakeside Ruins (Hunter Camp B)**

The road east from the Lower Road, now accessible. The lake basin begins here — the terrain drops slightly, the air changes, the vegetation becomes denser and damper. The ruins of the old temple complex are visible from the road.

Hunter Camp B occupies the Lakeside Ruins. Different hunters from Satya's camp — this group has been running trade from the lake basin long enough to know the ruins and treat them as infrastructure. They sell from what they can find and what they can carry. They do not ask where Natha is going.

**VIII. Lake Interior / BOSS: Dedari → Mystic Sash**

The inner basin of the lake. The statue of the Dedari stands in the center — visible from the shore, the figure of a celestial nymph, beautiful and still. There is no sash on the statue. There is only the statue, and then the lake moving.

The Dedari was a celestial being who came through during a previous cycle and did not return in time. The boundary closed. She was stranded in the material world. Over time — decades, possibly longer — a physical body formed around her. Beautiful above the waterline. The corruption of Rangda's land built the rest. She is still partially herself. She remembers what she was. But the material body acts on instinct and the instinct is not hers.

She does not want to fight. But the body does.

Destroying the material form is not killing her. It is the only way to release her. The body dissolves. For a moment — just a moment — her true form is visible, ascending, present. In that last lucid instant before she is fully free, she sees the ceremonial cloth drifting around Natha and recognizes it for what it is. A celestial being recognizing something sacred that its wearer does not yet understand.

She gives him the mantra. The correct words to speak to the cloth. Not a trade. Not a reward. Grace, offered freely by something returning home.

After the mantra, the cloth responds. The Mystic Sash that has been drifting passively around Natha since before the mask becomes what it always was — an extension of himself, finally understood.

The Upper Road and the Cliff Feet Temple, both previously inaccessible, are now reachable.

**IX. Upper Road (Mystic Sash Required)**

The elevated path above the Crossroads, running through the upper terrace zone. The view from here shows the full scope of the Middle Ground — the corrupted fields below, the road network, the lake basin, the cliff face ahead. It is the first moment the map becomes legible as a whole.

Accessible from the Upper Road:
- **Corrupted Village 2** to the west — another slave operation, same pattern as the Crossroads, different village. The scale of what Rangda has built is visible here. It was not one village. It was systematic.
- **Hunter Camp C** to the east — the last hunter settlement before the cliff approach. Sells equipment suited for the terrain above.
- **Spirit Beast Zone (Forest Depth)** — the deep forest, accessible from the Upper Road. Spirit beasts are more aggressive here and more physically present. A shortcut back down to the Forest Edge runs through this zone for players willing to navigate it.
- **Abandoned Temple (Prologue)** — the temple Natha fell from. Accessible now from the ground.

**X. Abandoned Temple (Prologue)**

From the inside it is different. He fell from somewhere above here — the cliff edge is accessible now, visible from below, the height of it not abstract anymore. Whatever put him in the water started here. The temple is old, older than the occupation, and something of significance was left inside.

**XI. Temple Depths**

Below the Abandoned Temple, accessible through the interior. A shortcut back to the Middle Ground Entry runs through here — the first major loop of the Middle Ground, the map folding back on itself. Players who find this early will reach the Shore Temple Gate again. Players who find it late will be glad it is there.

**XII. Ritual Ground / Pura Dalem — Sayan**

The Ritual Ground sits within the Pura Dalem complex — the Temple of the Dead, built at the eastern edge of the Middle Ground where the territory transitions toward the lake. The Pura Dalem is older than the current kingdom and has a function specific to death rites and the boundary between the material world and what is beyond it. Rangda's forces do not occupy it. Not out of respect — out of the practical recognition that the Pura Dalem is not useful to an occupation's supply logistics, and whatever lives in the margins of a place like this is not worth the cost of finding out.

Sayan is here.

He arrived before Natha did. He has been watching the decay of the kingdom from this position — the supply routes visible from the Ritual Ground elevation, the corrupted villages audible in the early morning, the Pura Dalem's strange quiet a better thinking space than the roads. He came for Rangda. He has been here long enough to have mapped what he can see and to know that he cannot do what needs doing alone.

He has also been here long enough to know what Rangda's underlings look like. And whatever is moving through this territory wearing a mask and carrying impossible weapons looks, to Sayan's assessment, exactly like something that serves the witch queen.

He attacks without warning.

The fight at the Ritual Ground is fast and serious. Sayan is peak human — no supernatural enhancement, just years of hunting things that should have killed him. He does not hold back. He does not perform. He commits, and Natha has to match it.

When it settles — when both of them have established that the other is not going to die easily and not going to stop — there is a pause. Sayan reads the situation the way he reads everything: quickly, from new information, without attachment to the previous conclusion. The mask on Natha's face is not Rangda's. The way Natha survived that fight is not the way Rangda's servants survive.

He removes the Khon mask. No explanation. Just removes it. Shows his face. Puts it back on.

Then he listens.

Natha gives him the rebel base mantra — the access that lets him cross the Veil and reach Wikananda's people. Sayan takes it the way he takes most things: without visible gratitude, with complete intention to use it.

They do not become allies in any formal sense. They become two people who have stopped trying to kill each other and are going in the same direction.

Sayan shows up again. He does not announce when he will.

**XIII. Coastal Cliff**

The western approach to the cliff face. Rangda's forces maintain a presence here — the cliff road is the main route between the Middle Ground and anything above it. The Coastal Cliff area sits between the flat terrain of the fields and the beginning of the vertical ascent.

**XIV. Cliff Feet Temple / Interior**

The temple at the base of the cliff — accessible from the Upper Road (Mystic Sash Required). Used originally as a waypoint temple for pilgrims ascending to the higher temples above. Currently fortified by Rangda's forces as a checkpoint on the cliff road.

The interior leads upward to the Cliff Approach.

The boss holding the passage to the Cliff Approach and, beyond it, Cliff Temple.

## Key Characters

**Satya** — At Hunter Camp A. Does not approach first. Transaction-based cooperation. Quest: kill the Overseer, receive rebel base mantra. Relocates to base afterward. Sells bow techniques and supplies. Holds the belief that the villagers can be cured without telling anyone he believes it.

**Sayan** — At the Ritual Ground. Arrived independently. Witch hunter, Sak Yant-covered, wearing the lower half of a Khon mask. Has been mapping the occupation from a distance. Attacks Natha immediately on sight — mistakes him for Rangda's servant. After the fight settles, removes his mask briefly, receives the rebel base mantra from Natha. Meeting Natha is the first confirmation he has had that Rangda is actually killable. Sells techniques and information. Returns at key moments.

**Hunters (Camp B, Camp C)** — No names currently established. Independent traders. Sell goods without questions. Their camps are landmarks more than characters — reliable safe points in a zone that is otherwise occupied or corrupted territory.
